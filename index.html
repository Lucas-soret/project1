Type of Bees

The Solitary Bee

Solitary bees, as their name suggests, do not live in colonies like honeybees but instead nest individually. They can be found in a variety of habitats, including forests, grasslands, and gardens. Unlike honeybees, solitary bees do not produce honey and they are not aggressive, as they do not have a colony to defend. Instead, solitary bees are focused on gathering nectar and pollen to feed their young.

The Queen Bee

Her main mission is to laying eggs to maintain the hive poppulation. Biggest individu of the group, she is usualy located in the center of the hive, Her pheromones help to keep the hive's social structure in order.

The Worker Bees

Non reproductives females they represent the majority of the hive. They are in charge of collecting nectare and pollen, cleaning the hive, caring for the young bees and also protecting the hive of intruder.

The Drones

The male bees are stingerless and their only job in the hive is to mate with the queen bee.
